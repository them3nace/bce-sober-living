Admissions & Getting Started

To be eligible for residency at BCE Sober Living, you must:

  • Be committed to maintaining sobriety from alcohol and all illicit drugs
  • Complete a pre-admission interview
  • Have completed or be currently enrolled in treatment (detox, inpatient, or outpatient)
  • Be willing to participate in recovery activities (12-step meetings, therapy, etc.)
  • Agree to random drug and alcohol screening
  • Be able to maintain employment or actively seek employment
  • Respect house rules and fellow residents

The application process is simple:

  1. Contact us via phone at (424) 395-6144 or through our contact form
  2. Complete a brief phone screening to ensure we're a good fit
  3. Schedule an in-person or virtual interview
  4. Complete the application paperwork
  5. Upon approval, coordinate your move-in date

We strive to make the process as quick and smooth as possible, often completing it within 24-48 hours.

Absolutely! We encourage prospective residents and their families to schedule a tour. Contact us to arrange a visit at a time that works for you. During the tour, you'll see our facilities, meet staff members, and have the opportunity to ask questions.

Cost & Payment

Our pricing is competitive and reflects the quality of our facilities and services. Costs typically include:

  • Monthly rent (contact us for current rates)
  • Security deposit (usually equal to one month's rent)
  • Utilities (included in monthly rent)
  • WiFi (included)

We offer transparent pricing with no hidden fees. Contact us for detailed pricing information and payment plans.

While sober living housing is typically not covered by insurance, some insurance plans may offer benefits for transitional housing. We can provide documentation to submit to your insurance company. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to inquire about any available benefits.

We understand that financial concerns can be a barrier to recovery. We work with residents to establish payment arrangements when possible. Please contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Living at BCE

Our house rules are designed to create a safe, supportive environment:

  • Zero tolerance for alcohol or drug use
  • Mandatory participation in recovery activities
  • Curfew times (with some flexibility for work schedules)
  • Random drug and alcohol testing
  • Mandatory house meetings
  • Respect for shared spaces and fellow residents
  • Employment or active job search required
  • Guest policies (limited visiting hours)

There is no set minimum or maximum length of stay. Most residents stay between 3-12 months, but everyone's recovery journey is unique. We work with each resident to determine the best timeline for their situation. Some may need only a few months, while others benefit from a longer stay.

Yes, visitors are allowed during designated hours and in common areas only. All visitors must be approved in advance and follow house guidelines. Overnight guests are not permitted to maintain the safety and structure of our sober living environment.

We understand that recovery is a journey with challenges. If a resident relapses, they must be honest and immediately inform staff. Depending on the circumstances, options may include:

  • Returning to a higher level of care (detox or inpatient treatment)
  • Temporary leave with the possibility of return after completing treatment
  • Increased support and monitoring

Our priority is always the safety and well-being of all residents. We approach each situation with compassion while maintaining our zero-tolerance policy for active use in the home.

Support & Services

BCE Sober Living provides comprehensive support including:

  • 24/7 staff availability for emergencies
  • Regular house meetings and check-ins
  • Peer support from fellow residents
  • Connections to local recovery resources
  • Employment assistance and job search support
  • Life skills development
  • Accountability and structure
  • Referrals to therapists, counselors, and medical providers

Residents are expected to arrange their own transportation for work, meetings, and appointments. However, we're located near public transportation and can provide information about local transit options. We may assist with transportation for house-related activities when possible.

Residents are responsible for their own meals. Our fully equipped kitchens allow residents to prepare their own food, which is an important part of developing independent living skills. Common areas are available for communal meals if residents choose to cook together.

Location & Accessibility

We're located in Frederick, Maryland, a historic city offering the perfect balance of community support and access to resources. Frederick provides:

  • Multiple recovery meetings throughout the area
  • Quality healthcare facilities
  • Employment opportunities
  • Public transportation options
  • Beautiful parks and outdoor recreation
  • A supportive recovery community

Yes, our location is accessible via Frederick's public bus system. We can provide information about routes and schedules during your tour or interview.
